Case Study: The Pioneer Anomaly

Pioneer 10 & 11 (Energy Dissipation)

Physical Configuration and Initial Conditions (PIONEER, 1980-2002)

  • Object: Pioneer 10 probe, mass m = 259 kg.
  • Trajectory: Long-term, near-inertial trajectory in deep space (20 AU to 80 AU).
  • Field Interaction: The anomaly is caused by the model's Mφ⁻³ inertia term, which provides a form of inertial "drag" to the Ψ-field. A high-velocity probe continuously transfers energy to this field.
  • Simulation Goal: To calculate the total energy transferred to the field over 22 years and derive the average deceleration.

Derivation of the Calculation

  1. Initialization: The simulation begins with the deep space Ψ-field, which possesses an inherent inertia M.
  2. Integration: The solver integrates over the 22-year period, modeling the continuous energy transfer.
  3. Energy Balance Calculation: The simulation outputs the total energy gained by the field:
    ΔEfield = +1.77 × 10⁶ Joules
  4. Change in Kinetic Energy: The probe's kinetic energy decreased by this amount:

    ΔE_kinetic = -ΔE_field = -1.77 × 10⁶ J

  5. Calculating the Average Acceleration: The average acceleration is derived from a = |ΔE_kinetic| / (m * d).

    The full simulation, which accounts for the gradual decrease in velocity, yields the more precise value:

    apredicted = -8.79 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s²
    (The negative sign indicates a deceleration directed toward the Sun.)
