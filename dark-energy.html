Case Study : Accelerated Expansion

The Dark Energy Problem

Mathematical Description of the Problem

Standard Model (Friedmann Equation)

The temporal evolution of the universe's scale factor (a) is described by the Friedmann equation. To explain acceleration (ä > 0), an extra term, the cosmological constant (Λ), is required:

ä/a = -4πG/3 * (ρ + 3p) + Λ/3

For acceleration to occur, the Λ term must dominate over gravitational attraction.

The Problem

Λ represents a "dark energy" of unknown origin, and its theoretical value is wrong by 120 orders of magnitude.

The Dephaze Model Derivation

In the Dephaze model, acceleration is an apparent effect arising from the discrepancy between the universe's fundamental timescale (s = ln(t_phase)) and our measured, local clock time (t).

Fundamental vs. Measured Time

Let's assume the universe's scale factor (a) expands linearly in the system's natural phase time (s). This represents the simplest state after the Φ³ projection:

da/ds = Hs (a constant expansion rate in phase time)

The Inertia of Time

The d/d(ln t_phase) term in the master equation implies that the "speed" of physical processes (our atomic clocks, our biology) is not constant relative to phase time. Due to the system's "time-inertia," local clock time (t) passes progressively slower compared to fundamental phase time (s). Mathematically, this means the derivative ds/dt is an increasing function. We can assume the simplest increasing relationship:

ds/dt = k * t (where k is a constant)

Derivation of Apparent Acceleration

Using the chain rule, we can express the measured velocity (ȧ = da/dt) and the measured acceleration (ä = d²a/dt²) in terms of our clock time:

ȧ = da/dt = (da/ds) * (ds/dt) = Hs * (k*t)

This gives the measured expansion velocity as:

ȧ(t) = (Hs*k) * t

This shows that the measured expansion velocity increases linearly with time. Differentiating with respect to t one more time gives:

ä = dȧ/dt = Hs * k = const > 0

This result demonstrates that in a universe expanding linearly in its own natural time, its expansion will appear to be constantly accelerating when measured by our "slowing" clocks.
